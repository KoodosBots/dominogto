Play Dominoes

Your goal in this version of dominoes is to earn the target number of points before your opponent. Each session consists of several rounds of domino games. The winner of each round receives the value of the opponent ’s remaining dominoes.

  • Choose either Block or Draw modes, and how many players you want in the game (from 2 to 4).
  • Choose a goal point total (100, 150, or 200).
  • Whoever discards all their dominoes or has the lowest value of remaining dominoes wins the round.
  • Play through as many rounds as needed to complete the game.

Play Dominoes Game for Kids Video Game Play Instructions

How to Play

Discard dominoes in turns until you or your opponent run out of dominoes or have no legal moves available. Earn points based on the value of your opponents ’dominoes. Earn a specific number of points after several rounds to win.

  • Starting the Game
    • On the welcome screen are the following controls:
      • Select the play button on the bottom center to begin.
      • Select the gear button on the top right corner to open the following options:
        • Speaker button: Turns the game ’s sound effects on and off
        • Music button: Turns the game ’s music on and off
        • Enlarge button: Lets you switch between windowed and full screen modes
  • Game Controls
    • Desktop
      • With your mouse, left click to select.
    • Touchscreen devices
      • Tap on the screen with your fingers to select.
    • Controls and indicators
      • Top right corner:
        • Gear button
          • Speaker button
          • Music button
          • Enlarge button
  • Game Play
    • Setting up
      • Choose a game mode:
        • Block dominoes
        • Draw dominoes
      • Choose your score goal
        • 100 points
        • 150 points
        • 200 points
      • Choose the number of players:
        • 2 players
        • 3 players
        • 4 players
      • Choose a domino style. There are 5 styles to choose from.
    • Basic rules
      • Each player begins with 7 dominoes.
        • The player with the double domino of the highest value (e.g. 6/6 or 5/5) goes first.
        • The player going first will discard their highest-value double domino.
        • The next player in line will connect any of the dominoes they have to the first player ’s domino.
      • To connect and discard dominoes you must choose one whose numbers match the domino whose ends are open.
        • Free ends are dominos that have one side that ’s not connected to another domino. There are two of these in every game.
        • For instance, if the free ends of a domino set have a value of five and a three, you can only discard dominoes with a five or a three on them.
        • The game will helpfully highlight which dominoes you can discard and which end you can place them.
      • The moment you can ’t discard any domino, the game ’s rules vary:
        • In Block Mode, you lose a turn the moment you run out of moves. Your opponents get another move.
          • You don ’t get a move until you can find a domino you can discard.
          • If your opponents don ’t have a move, either, the round ends.
          • The player whose remaining dominoes have the smallest total dot value wins the round.
        • In Draw Mode, you don ’t lose a turn.
          • Instead, you must draw a new domino from the graveyard until you have a piece you can play. The same applies for your opponent.
          • Once you run out of dominoes, you lose a turn.
      • Keep discarding dominoes until no valid moves remain.
      • There are two ways to win the round:
        • Be the first player to discard all the dominoes.
        • When no more moves are allowed, have the dominoes with the lowest value among the players.
    • Rounds and scoring
      • Each session is divided into rounds.
        • After every round, the winner receives the number of points derived from the value of the remaining dominoes in the other players ’deck.
        • If, for instance, your opponent has dominoes with a value of 10 when taken together, you earn 10 points.
      • The game ends when you reach the target score
        • The target score is determined at the start of the game.
        • Because you can earn so few points per round, there is no upper limit to how many rounds it ’ll take to win.
          • In general, games with 2 players take longer than games with 4 players.
          • Block games are also faster paced than Draw games.
          • If you want a long match, set the target score to the highest available number, 200.
      • When you win, you can send the game to your friends on Facebook, Twitter, and WhatsApp.
  • Tips
    • Play the doubles early.
      • You have fewer opportunities to play them, as the other domino options can match on either side.
      • Getting rid of them early on usually saves you the headache of trying to play them later.
    • Play your heaviest dominoes.
      • The game automatically recommends this if you go first.
      • Ditching the biggest dominoes early in the game will help tilt the odds in your favor in two ways:
        • Big tiles are harder to discard. Ditching them sooner saves you a lot of trouble later in the round.
        • At the end of the game, having smaller-valued dominoes tilts the score in your favor.
        • Even if you lose the round, the smaller value your opponent receives will buy you time you need to catch up.
    • Play the slow game.
      • By slowing down, you can work on analyzing your opponents ’weaknesses and where your strengths and weaknesses lie.
      • This is especially noticeable in draw games.
      • You can then figure out likely counters.
    • Play your strong suit.
      • Your strong suit is the number you have the most. By that logic, your opponent is likely to have fewer of them.
      • By drawing more of them you increase the odds of blocking your opponent or forcing them to
  • Target Audience
    • The game is suited for players who like simple dominoes games.
    • The game has no adult themed content.
